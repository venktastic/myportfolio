AI-Leveraged Observation Workflow (Predictive Safety)

Detail Description
Role Product Manager
Domain Artificial Intelligence, Health, Safety & Environment (HSE)
The Big Picture Automated the manual safety observation triage process using an AI model to predict Hazard, Severity, and Root Cause elements just with frontline worker's description of a safety event in real-time. This strategic intervention dramatically reduced the time from reporting to rectification by X%, moving the organization from reactive to predictive safety management.
Key Skills Showcased AI/ML Product Definition, Discovery, UI/UX Design, Stakeholder Alignment, Roadmap and Prioritization.

The Challenge: Latency in Proactive Safety

In construction and industrial environments, frontline workers (Contractors/Subcontractors) submit hundreds of daily safety observations via mobile apps. The core challenge was transforming this high volume of unstructured data (photos, text descriptions) into actionable intelligence fast enough to prevent incidents.

Stakeholder Pain Points

  • HSE Managers: Overwhelmed by volume, spending excessive time manually reading, classifying, prioritizing, and assigning observations. This led to critical observations being missed or delayed.
  • Project Management Consultants (PMC): Lacked real-time visibility into leading risk indicators across the project, making it difficult to prioritize high-risk sites or allocate resources effectively.
  • Frontline Workers: Experienced slow feedback loops (72+ hours), decreasing their motivation to report proactively.

Target Goal: Eliminate manual triage time and drastically reduce the exposure time to identified risks.

The Solution: AI-Powered Risk Triage Engine

We developed the AI Observation Workflow, which automatically processes mobile reports and predicts critical risk parameters in real-time, shifting the safety team's focus from classification to rectification.

Workflow Comparison: Manual vs. AI-Leveraged

Workflow Step Traditional Manual Process AI-Leveraged Workflow
Data Capture Reporter submits data via mobile form. Reporter submits data via mobile app (text, photo, location).
Risk Triage HSE Manager reviews the report, assigns Hazard, Severity, and Root Cause codes. (Time: 30+ min) AI Engine performs real-time classification and prediction of Hazard Type, Severity (High/Medium/Low), and Probable Root Cause. (Time: < 5 sec)
Assignment HSE Manager manually routes the observation. System automatically assigns the observation to the relevant field supervisor based on AI-predicted severity and location.
Rectification Reviewer rectifies the issue. Reviewer acts on a pre-classified, high-priority task. HSE Manager only reviews high-severity, AI-flagged items.

My Role as Product Manager: Defining the Predictive Layer

I served as the lead PM for this initiative, responsible for defining and delivering the end-to-end predictive experience.

  • Strategy & Vision: Defined the product vision around "zero-touch safety classification," ensuring the AI model was trained specifically on past observation, near-miss, and incident data to maximize its predictive value.
  • Data & ML Integration: Collaborated closely with the Data Science team to define the inference service API contract and validate the model's accuracy, focusing on maximizing the precision of High Severity predictions to minimize false positives and maintain user trust.
  • UX/UI: Designed the mobile interface for clear, fast reporting, and the Reviewer dashboard to present AI-predicted classifications (e.g., "AI Predicted Severity: High") alongside the critical option for manual override.
  • Stakeholder Alignment: Conducted workshops with senior PMCs and HSE Managers to align on the operational definition of "Hazard" and "Root Cause" to ensure model output was practically useful and immediately actionable in the field.

4. Outcome and Impact: Shift to Proactive Risk Management

The implementation of the AI-Leveraged Observation Workflow resulted in a significant shift toward proactive risk management, delivering massive gains in efficiency and safety outcomes.

Key Measurable Outcomes

Metric Baseline Result Impact
Time to Rectification 72 hours 18 hours 75% reduction in time from report to assignment/rectification, drastically lowering exposure time to risk.
HSE Manager Triage Time 40% of their day spent on manual triage 10% of their day Reduced time by 75%, allowing them to focus on site audits and strategic training.
Observation Closure Rate 65% 88% Increased engagement and accountability among field supervisors due to clear, prioritized tasks.
Critical Near Misses X per month 25% decrease Validated the predictive power of the system by reducing incidents related to common AI-identified hazards (e.g., "poor housekeeping").

Learnings & Next Steps

The success of the Triage Engine validated the need for ML-driven workflows in operational environments. The primary learning was that user trust in AI is directly correlated with the accuracy of the high-severity predictions—it's better to have high precision (fewer false alarms) than high recall (catching everything).

Next Step: Extend the trained model to process image data (photos submitted by reporters) to automatically identify and classify hazards, further reducing reliance on human text input.
